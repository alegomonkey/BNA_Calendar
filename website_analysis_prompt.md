# Website Analysis & Improvement Prompt

---

## THE PROMPT

You are an expert web designer, UX strategist, and WordPress developer. I need you to analyze two websites and produce a concrete, prioritized improvement plan for my site.

---

### SITE TO IMPROVE

**URL:** [Community Calendar – Bayside Neighborhood Association](https://baysideportland.org/community-calendar/)

**Purpose / elevator pitch:** A community calendar with a schedule view for active events in the neighborhood. 

**Current problems I'm already aware of:** Its a google calendar drop in - We want to restyle while maintaining linkage to google calandar

**My WordPress.com plan:** Personal

**Theme I'm using:** Lovecraft

---

### REFERENCE SITE

**URL:** [Queer Events Calendar | Portland Maine | ECC — Equality Community Center](https://eccmaine.org/calendar?view=calendar&month=05-2026)

**Why I chose it as a reference:** The layout is clean and each event in the calendar links to a page with more info on the event. Additionally all events can be added to google calendar or Ical from the info page. 

**Specific elements I want to borrow or adapt:** Navigation structure

---

### MY REQUIREMENTS

The following requirements are intentionally vague — please interpret each one, ask clarifying questions where critical, and propose specific solutions for each:

1. List things I and others add to google calendar √
2. Not look like total garbage
3. Remain in schedule view (not month view like the ECC) √
4. Link each event on the schedule to a page with details from the google calendar
5. Allow people to add the event to their own ical or google calendar
6. Have room for additional info, photos or links for select events

---

### WHAT I WANT FROM YOU

Please do the following in order:

**1. Site Audit (Site to Improve)**
Analyze my site across these dimensions and give each a score (1–10) with a one-sentence explanation:
- Visual design & brand consistency
- Navigation & information architecture
- Mobile responsiveness
- Page load & performance signals
- Calls to action / conversion
- Content clarity
- Typography & readability

**2. Reference Site Breakdown**
Identify the 5–7 specific techniques, design patterns, or structural choices that make the reference site effective and worth emulating.

**3. Requirements Interpretation**
For each of my vague requirements, state:
- What you interpret it to mean concretely
- Whether any clarification is needed before acting on it
- What the ideal solution would look like

**4. Prioritized Improvement Plan**
Produce a numbered list of improvements, ordered by impact-to-effort ratio (highest first). For each item include:
- **What to change** (specific, not vague)
- **Why it matters**
- **How to do it in WordPress.com** (within my plan's limitations — note if a plan upgrade is needed)
- **Estimated effort:** Low / Medium / High
- **Inspired by reference site?** Yes / No

**5. WordPress.com Constraints Check**
Flag any improvements that are blocked by WordPress.com limitations (vs. self-hosted WordPress.org) and suggest the best available workaround or whether a plan upgrade unlocks it.

**6. Quick Wins**
Call out the top 3 changes I can make today, in under 30 minutes each, that will have an immediate visible impact.

- I have full admin access to the WordPress.com site

- Budget is zero
- The calendar in schedule view must be updatable through google calendar. 
- My audience is: local small business owners, 35–55, not very tech-savvy
